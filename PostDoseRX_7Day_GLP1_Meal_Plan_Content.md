## Page 3: GLP-1 Nutrition Basics

### How GLP-1 Medications Affect Your Eating

**Key Changes You May Experience:**
- Reduced appetite and feeling full faster
- Delayed gastric emptying (food stays in stomach longer)  
- Potential nausea, especially early in treatment
- Changes in food preferences and cravings

**Nutrition Principles for Success:**
1. **Eat Protein First** - 20-30g per meal maintains muscle
2. **Choose Nutrient-Dense Foods** - Make every bite count
3. **Stay Hydrated** - Aim for 64+ oz water daily
4. **Eat Slowly** - Give your body time to register fullness
5. **Avoid Trigger Foods** - High-fat, spicy, or very sweet foods may worsen nausea

## Page 4: Meal Timing Guide

### When to Eat with Your GLP-1 Medication

**Daily Injection Schedule (Ozempic, Wegovy):**
- Take medication same time each week
- No specific meal timing requirements
- Focus on consistent daily eating pattern

**Suggested Daily Schedule:**
- **7:00 AM** - Light breakfast (within 2 hours of waking)
- **12:00 PM** - Lunch (main meal of the day)
- **3:00 PM** - Protein snack (if needed)
- **6:00 PM** - Light dinner (finish 3 hours before bed)

**Weekly Injection Schedule (Mounjaro):**
- Take same day each week
- Can be taken with or without food
- Plan lighter meals on injection day if experiencing nausea

## Page 14: Managing Side Effects

### Eating Strategies for Common GLP-1 Side Effects

**Nausea Management:**
- Start with bland foods (toast, crackers, rice)
- Eat small, frequent meals
- Avoid greasy, spicy, or very sweet foods
- Ginger tea may help settle stomach
- Don't lie down immediately after eating

**Constipation Relief:**
- Increase fiber gradually
- Drink plenty of water
- Include prunes or fiber-rich fruits
- Light exercise after meals
- Consider magnesium supplement (consult doctor)

**Reduced Appetite:**
- Focus on protein and nutrients first
- Don't force large meals
- Liquid nutrition (smoothies) may be easier
- Set eating reminders
- Make every calorie count with nutrient-dense foods

**Food Aversions:**
- Keep a list of tolerated foods
- Try different textures (smooth vs. crunchy)
- Temperature matters (cold foods may be easier)
- Stick to familiar, comforting foods initially

## Page 15: Hydration & Supplements

### Staying Hydrated on GLP-1 Medications

**Daily Hydration Goal:** 64+ ounces

**Hydration Tips:**
- Start each day with a large glass of water
- Sip throughout the day (don't wait until thirsty)
- Flavor water with lemon, cucumber, or mint
- Herbal teas count toward fluid intake
- Monitor urine color (pale yellow is ideal)

**Electrolyte Balance:**
- Include sodium in your diet (you may eat less overall)
- Potassium from fruits and vegetables
- Magnesium supports muscle function

**Supplement Considerations:**
*Always consult your healthcare provider before starting any supplements*

**Commonly Discussed:**
- Multivitamin (insurance for reduced food intake)
- Vitamin B12 (if energy levels drop)
- Fiber supplement (for constipation)
- Probiotics (for digestive health)

## Page 17: Recipe Substitutions

### Adapting Recipes for GLP-1 Success

**Protein Swaps:**
- Chicken → Fish, lean beef, tofu
- Beef → Turkey, bison, plant protein
- Dairy → Greek yogurt, cottage cheese

**Carb Modifications:**
- Regular pasta → Zucchini noodles, shirataki
- White rice → Cauliflower rice, quinoa
- Bread → Lettuce wraps, portobello caps

**Cooking Method Swaps:**
- Fried → Baked, grilled, air-fried
- Heavy sauces → Herbs, spices, light vinaigrettes
- Large portions → Half portions, save leftovers

**Nausea-Friendly Alternatives:**
- Hot foods → Room temperature or cold
- Spicy → Mild with fresh herbs
- Greasy → Lean proteins with light seasonings
- Sweet → Fresh fruit instead of desserts
